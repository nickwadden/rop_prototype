You can make opportunities for young people of Afrikan descent.

Studies show that young people of Afrikan descent are being held back by poverty, violence and low education. In Toronto 140,000 young people of Afrikan decent are being excluded from mainstream prosperity, particularly those who live in one of Toronto’s 13 “priority neighbourhoods” identified by United Way Toronto’s “Poverty by Postal Code” report.

Rites of Passage helps young people find their purpose in life, contribute positively to their community and find the resiliency to navigate their turbulent lives. Rites of Passage has proved life-changing results:

  • 100% of the youth found and kept meaningful summer employment and many have continued in full time and part time work, opening economic opportunities. This also showed the youth that they are welcome in Toronto and not “stuck” in their neighbourhoods.
  • Improvement in emotional competence as indicated in EQi testing. The EQi reports on 5 major emotional areas of competence: Intrapersonal, Interpersonal, Stress Management, Adaptability, and General Mood. Initial results suggest participants score higher after participating in Rites of Passage.
  • 75% of the youth demonstrated their commitment to being community leaders by volunteering their time in community projects after Rites of Passage.

Event Details

Rites of Passage brings 20 young people of African descent into responsible adulthood every year. Based in Parma Court, a social housing community in Victoria Village, one of Toronto’s “priority neighbourhoods”, this 12 month program is based on a well-tested, internationally recognized afro-centric curriculum and includes volunteering, leadership training and employment.

Leadership skills are instilled in the young people using four pillars with their roots in the Akan tradition of West Africa. These are: Nurturing Responsible Adulthood – Young people who participate in the program are asked to reflect on what it means to be an adult and to work towards this vision. Developing a Sense of Purpose – Through guided introspection and discussion, participants develop goals using their own values. Building a Community Perspective – The kids are encouraged to take their strong sense of community, and translate this into action. Fostering Resiliency – Feelings of self-esteem are important to young adults, but particularly crucial in a group that is treated by society and the media as having less potential. Exercises build up a sense of self-worth, which will drive participants towards long-term personal growth, and success.
